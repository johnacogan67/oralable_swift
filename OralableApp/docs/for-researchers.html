For Researchers

Temporalis optical myography (OMG), structured exports, and collaborative motion-artifact science.

McGill University collaboration

We work with McGill University on methods to reduce motion artifact and improve interpretability of cheek-placed PPG during sleep. That partnership informs our filtering choices, classifier validation, and trial protocol design—not a substitute for independent peer review, but a core part of how we stress-test Temporalis pipelines before field deployment.

Technical capabilities

Real-time raw CSV export

50 Hz-aligned Oralable lanes (PPG, accelerometry, derived channels) for offline analysis and institutional tooling.

Charlton framework signal processing

Pulse waveform analytics and band-limited PPG processing consistent with the Charlton pulse-wave literature, supporting HOI / temporalis load interpretation.

Pearson r: SASHB correlation

Session and hourly rollups expose hypoxic burden (SASHB) alongside Temporalis rescue fraction for smoking-gun style correlation (Pearson r) in trial analytics.
